## ğŸ” How to Find Your Schedule

### For Eskom Direct Customers:

1. Visit [loadshedding.eskom.co.za](https://loadshedding.eskom.co.za/)
2. Search for your suburb or area
3. Note your schedule block number
4. Check the current stage to see when you'll be affected

### For Municipal Customers:

1. Visit [Eskom Municipal Schedules](https://www.eskom.co.za/distribution/customer-service/outages/municipal-loadshedding-schedules/)
2. Select your province
3. Click on your municipality
4. Download your area's schedule

**Note:** If you receive your monthly account from a municipality (not Eskom directly), you must use your municipality's schedule.

## ğŸ“Š Understanding Load Shedding Stages

Load shedding stages are cumulative:

- **Stage 1:** 1,000 MW shed - least amount of outages
- **Stage 2:** 2,000 MW shed - includes Stage 1 times
- **Stage 3:** 3,000 MW shed - includes Stage 1 & 2 times
- **Stage 4:** 4,000 MW shed - includes all previous stages
- **Stage 5:** 5,000 MW shed - more frequent outages
- **Stage 6:** 6,000 MW shed - maximum frequency
- **Stage 7-8:** Emergency measures with extended outages

**Important:** Higher stages include all lower stage times, meaning more frequent power cuts.

## ğŸ’¡ Tips for Managing Load Shedding

### Preparation:
- Install at least one reliable app (EskomSePush recommended)
- Set up notifications for your area
- Keep your phone charged
- Have backup lighting ready (torches, lamps, candles)

### During Load Shedding:
- Turn off sensitive electronics before power goes out
- Use surge protectors for expensive equipment
- Keep freezer/fridge closed to maintain temperature
- Have a power bank for charging devices

### Planning:
- Check your schedule weekly
- Plan cooking times around outages
- Schedule work tasks during power availability
- Consider inverter/UPS for critical equipment
